# titles — fern study

Unlike withhold-the-noun channels, fern NAMES the subject and lets scale
words + institutions carry the pull. The promise is "an epic true
story told properly", not a tease.

## Rules (evidence: 20 ranked candidates, views 6.4M-21M)

1. **Four formulas cover the catalog; rotate, never repeat back-to-back:**
   - THE HUNT FOR X — manhunt frame. "The Hunt for the King of the Dark
     Web" (16M), "...America's Smartest Killer" (10M), "...Ted Bundy"
     (7.9M), "...El Chapo" (7.2M).
   - HOW A/AN X DID Y — mechanism frame. "How an FBI Agent Infiltrated the
     KKK" (13M), "How a Pilot Hijacked a Plane" (8.1M), "How Hackers Stole
     $1,000,000,000 From Banks" (6.5M).
   - THE X WHO DID Y — person frame. "The Kids Who Hacked The CIA" (19M),
     "The Kids Who Hacked Xbox" (10M).
   - SUPERLATIVE + CATEGORY — scale frame. "The Biggest Nuke in History"
     (7M), "The World's Craziest Prison Escape" (6.4M), "The Genius Design
     of Narco Submarines" (8M).
2. **A superlative or institution appears in almost every title**: Smartest,
   Deadliest, Biggest, Craziest, Genius / CIA, FBI, KKK, Interpol, Bin
   Laden. One per title, never stacked.
3. **4-9 words, Title Case, no numbers-as-listicle, no question marks**
   except genuine mysteries ("What Was on Bin Laden's Hard Drive?", 6.7M).
4. **Money and magnitude are written OUT for shock**: "$1,000,000,000"
   not "$1B".
5. **Occasional flat provocation** as a changeup: "South Korea Is a
   Dystopia" (6.5M) — a declarative judgment, no hedge.
6. Titles must still be TRUE; the video must pay the scale word off.

# Script skill — Serious History study (packs 1-2 confirmed; 3-5 refine)

Authoring addendum for channels trained on this study. Append to
`storyboard_v3_prompt.md` rules; where they conflict, the studio spec wins
(curiosity-gap title, wind-down ending, no em dashes).

## Structure (evidence: ee0f + ad91 chapters + timelines)

- Compilation format: 3-4 self-contained stories per video, ~5 min each,
  each with a chapter marker and a spoken segment intro ("Number 1.
  {Name}"). For our ~5 min sample: 3 stories at ~90-100s equivalent.
- COLD OPEN template, verbatim shape: "Today, we're going over {premise
  restated from the title}." One sentence, then straight into story 1. A
  single casual aside is allowed ("Oh, and I'm back!") but never more.
- Each story: present-tense scene drop ("It's 1812, and a 12-year-old boy
  named John Brown is delivering a herd of cattle...") → character
  physicality early (height, weapon, clothes) → escalation → the death/
  payoff beat → one dry epilogue line.
- Facts carry the color; hedge legend as legend ("according to legend",
  "reputedly") but do not break stride for historiography.

## Register (evidence: both timelines)

- PRESENT TENSE for story action, past for context.
- Conversational connective tissue: "See, ...", "this guy is different",
  "it turns out", "gives off the vibes of".
- Wry superlative framing: "the greatest wingman in human history".
- Direct address sparingly; questions rare (their curiosity lives in the
  premise, not rhetorical questions).
- Sentences mostly 8-18 words; longer run-ons appear during action peaks.
- ~200 wpm delivery means text density is high: a 5-minute video needs
  ~950-1050 words.

## Our deltas (inspired, not copied)

- Keep the studio's flat-narrator rule: their energy comes from pace and
  wording, not exclamation. No hype words. Dry pivots.
- Our cold open still obeys the <=12-word rule: compress their template
  ("Today: the outlaws who said please.") rather than their longer form.
- Wind-down ending per studio rule (their videos end abruptly; ours
  settles in the last 2-3 scenes; this is a deliberate improvement).
- Every 20 s stands alone; context before event; re-anchor names after
  time jumps (studio flow rules stay law).

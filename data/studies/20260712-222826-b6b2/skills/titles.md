# Titles skill — study 20260712-222826-b6b2 (Serious History, 793k subs)

Evidence: the channel's COMPLETE catalog (58 long-form uploads scanned, the
top 30 ranked by views held in study.json candidates). This is a full-census
read, not a sample, so confidence is high on every rule below.

## The formula system (ranked by proven pull)

1. **"Most Epic {plural category} in History"** — the flagship. 8 of the
   top 30 use it (Deaths 6.2M, Revenge Stories 2.5M, Survival Stories 1.8M,
   Origin Stories 1.7M, Comebacks 1.3M, Last Stands 1.0M, plus "(pt. 2)"
   sequels). Superlative + emotional category + brand suffix.
2. **"Extreme {category} in/from History"** — the intensity variant
   (Vigilante Justice 5.9M, Stories of Revenge 2.6M, Betrayal 1.1M).
3. **"When {sympathetic subject} Snapped in History"** — the human-stakes
   reversal (Nice People 3.5M + 3.3M sequel; "When Alexander the Great Took
   Things Personally..." 1.4M). The comedic trail-off "..." is allowed here
   and only here.
4. **"Your Life as {historical figure}"** — second-person immersion
   (Miyamoto Musashi 3.5M on a 12.6m video, the best views-per-minute in
   the catalog; Adrian Carton de Wiart 1.5M). Strong but rarer.
5. **"{Superlative} {category} in History | {Specific Battle/Event}"** —
   the pipe pattern for single-subject epics (Cannae 1.8M, Agincourt 1.2M,
   Waterloo 1.2M). Consistently UNDERperforms the compilations; specific
   named subjects gate the click.
6. **Sequel markers**: "MORE {formula}" or "{formula} (pt. 2)" — they
   franchise whatever worked.

## Hard rules observed (cite: all 30 candidates)

- Title Case, always. No terminal punctuation except the comedic "...".
- NO listicle numbers, ever. "Most Epic", never "Top 10". Superlatives do
  the counting.
- "in History" / "from History" is the brand suffix: 21 of 30 titles.
- Emotional, human verbs (Snapped, Refused to Die, Took Things Personally)
  beat academic phrasing. People, not events, are the subject.
- 4-9 words, median 6. Shortest "Most Epic Deaths in History" (5), longest
  "The Worst Things Genghis Khan did to his Enemies" (9).
- Specific names appear ONLY in pipe subtitles or "Your Life as"; the main
  clause stays categorical so anyone can click.

## What we do BETTER (inspired, not copied)

Their titles promise a category; ours must also open a curiosity GAP
(studio rule 0). Fuse patterns: keep the superlative-category engine and
bend it toward a concrete paradox, e.g. "The Nicest Man in History Was a
One-Man Army" or "History's Most Polite Killers". Keep their word budget
(4-9), their no-numbers rule, their human verbs. Do not reuse "in History"
as a suffix on every title (that is their brand furniture); use it at most
every other video so ours reads adjacent, not cloned.

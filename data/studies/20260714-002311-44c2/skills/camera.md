# camera — fern study (skill pack #8; REPLACES parallax)

Motion mix measured across 1,744 shots (share of shots per video):

| motion | share | reading |
|---|---|---|
| handheld drift | 18-29% | the default "alive" state on footage and documents |
| static | 12-26% | inserts, cards, held evidence |
| zoom-in | 14-25% | approach, tension, focus onto a detail |
| zoom-out | 11-20% | reveals, scale, aftermath |
| pan L/R | 5-15% | space traversal, map moves |
| complex motion | 6-11% | reconstruction fly-throughs, in-footage action |

Roughly **three quarters of all shots move**, and the movement is SLOW.
Zooms outnumber pans about 2:1. Nothing whips.

## Types and where to use them (use_when -> prompt_words)

1. **Drone/establisher push-in** — chapter opens, new locations.
   prompt_words: "slow aerial push-in toward, cinematic drone shot"
2. **Detail zoom-in** — narration names a specific object/line/person.
   prompt_words: "slow push-in on, shallow focus, deliberate zoom"
3. **Reveal zoom-out** — aftermath, scale ("the largest manhunt"),
   consequence lines. prompt_words: "slow pull-back revealing, widening"
4. **Handheld document/evidence drift** — screenshots, papers, photos.
   prompt_words: "subtle handheld drift, slight sway, documentary feel"
5. **Lateral pan** — maps, timelines, moving between two anchored points.
   prompt_words: "slow lateral pan across, steady tracking"
6. **Static hold** — typeset cards, stamps, one-object inserts; also the
   held 20-55 s archival moments. prompt_words: "locked-off static shot"
7. **Reconstruction fly-through** — 3D space storytelling (the bc3b
   signature). prompt_words: "smooth camera glide through, orbiting"

Rules: one motion per shot; motion speed always "slow/subtle/deliberate";
match motion to narration function (approach=in, reveal=out, traverse=pan);
consecutive shots never repeat the same motion type more than twice.

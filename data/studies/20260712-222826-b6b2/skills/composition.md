# Composition skill — per-shot narration-to-visual mapping

Evidence: ee0f sheet 1 + timeline S001-S019; 98db sheet 1 + timeline
S001-S006; metrics from all 5 packs. Their composition grammar, annotated:

## The five moves (what is on screen and why)

1. **Mascot-hosted cold open.** The channel mascot stands on a flat void
   and performs the premise with animated presenter gestures (pose change
   about every second). WHY: instant brand recognition, zero art cost,
   frames the video as a show. (ee0f S001, 98db S001, identical.)
2. **Typeset segment cards.** "NUMBER 1" then the subject's name as plain
   dark typeset text on the void, exactly as spoken; optionally the name
   card sits beside a REAL historical artwork (Benkei woodblock print).
   WHY: chapters made visible; the historical insert lends authenticity to
   a cartoon. (Maps to our date chips + ref cards, already sanctioned.)
3. **Literal SHOW-the-line.** The drawing depicts the clause being spoken,
   concretely, not atmospherically: "you're born" = a baby in a cradle;
   "he towers over everyone" = wide 3-character height comparison;
   "a weapon, a cross between a sword and a long spear" = 0.8-1.2 s
   micro-cut close-ups landing ON the object as it is named. Sound-off
   test passes on nearly every shot.
4. **Identity anchors.** Each recurring character carries ONE loud visual
   feature that survives every age/costume change: Jeanne's dark-red hair
   (planted in the birth scene, paid off in the thumbnail), Benkei's size,
   the mascot's glasses. WHY: instant re-anchor after time jumps with zero
   spoken words. (Maps to our character bible + auto-filled `characters`.)
5. **Establishing mood drops.** A time/place jump opens with one moody
   painted wide (dark Kyoto street, lantern light) BEFORE characters act.
   WHY: signposts the jump (studio flow rule agrees).

## Rhythm rules

- Cut on the VISUAL subject change mid-sentence; ~11 words per shot in
  compilations.
- Micro-cut runs (2-3 shots of 0.8-1.2 s) reserved for named objects and
  reveals; do not overuse (5-10% of shots under 1 s).
- Long holds (20-45 s) allowed when a single tableau carries several story
  beats; camera drifts inside the hold (our parallax/Ken Burns covers).
- Backgrounds: soft painted, muted blues/browns/greys; characters pale
  expressive faces on simple dark limbs. Violence explicit but comic.

## Our deltas

- Our per-scene grain (one image per phrase) natively reproduces moves 3-5;
  the auto-director's subject-overlap lint enforces the sound-off test.
- Move 1: our bowler-hat mascot hosts cold opens on a cream void (NOT
  their grey void + glasses face).
- Move 2: typeset segment cards via Remotion/date-chip furniture; real
  historical art inserts via ref-cards with license-logged fetches.
- One dried-blood-red accent object per scene maximum (our channel
  signature; theirs is uncontrolled color).
